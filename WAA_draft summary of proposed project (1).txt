Summary of proposed project
Adults living with obesity are at risk of developing type 2 diabetes mellitus and coronary artery disease/cardiovascular diseases. Growing evidence suggest that chronic low-grade inflammation and immune system dysregulation are critical pathways linking obesity to T2DM and CVD. However, these immunoinflammatory-metabolic mechanisms are poorly characterised, and current prevention programmes rarely account for their contributions to differences in disease risk. Heavy reliance on BMI to define obesity in clinical practice does not capture the complex, multidimensional nature of obesity. Thus, limiting improved risk prediction models of T2DM and CVD in people living with obesity. Understanding the immunoinflammatory-metabolic variations inherent in obesity will contribute to improving obesity health outcomes and getting individuals timely assessed for the services and suitable treatment plan they need.
